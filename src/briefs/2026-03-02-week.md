## What changed

Microsoft published a guidance post on getting value out of the **Microsoft Sentinel data lake**, walking through how to centralize signals, retain years of telemetry cheaply, and run graph-powered analytics and agentic workflows over historical data. The pitch is aimed at teams that have been forced to drop or short-retain logs to control ingestion cost, which leaves investigation blind spots. On shift this matters when a hunt needs to reach back further than the analytics tier keeps: the data lake tier is where long-range correlation and retrospective IOC sweeps live, so knowing what it can (and can't) do shapes how you scope an investigation. ([Microsoft Sentinel Blog](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/microsoftsentinelblog/unlocking-value-with-microsoft-sentinel-data-lake/4497660))

Microsoft posted its **monthly security roundup for March 2, 2026**, consolidating the Defender and Sentinel changes that shipped across February. Highlights worth an intern's attention: the **UEBA behaviors layer in Sentinel reached GA** (plus a prebuilt behaviors workbook in the UEBA Essentials solution) that turns raw logs into human-readable "who did what to whom" summaries; several identity-focused **advanced hunting tables went GA** â€” `IdentityAccountInfo`, `EntraIdSignInEvents`, `EntraIdSpnSignInEvents`, and `GraphApiAuditEvents`; and **lake-only ingestion for Defender advanced hunting tables** is now GA, letting you land hunting data in the Sentinel data lake without paying to push it through the analytics tier. These are the tables and views you'll actually query when triaging identity incidents. ([Microsoft Defender XDR Blog](https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/microsoftthreatprotectionblog/monthly-news---march-2026/4498458))

## Worth knowing

The same roundup confirms Microsoft is **extending the sunset for managing Microsoft Sentinel in the Azure portal to March 31, 2027**. Sentinel is already GA in the Microsoft Defender portal (including for tenants without Defender XDR or an E5 license), so the unified portal is where new SOC work should land â€” but there's no forced cutover this week, and the extra runway gives migration planning breathing room rather than a scramble.
